Training & Fellowships

NIHR-funded predoctoral and postdoctoral fellowship programs at the Center for Health Outcomes and Policy Research, University of Pennsylvania School of Nursing.

Program Overview

The CHOPR Fellowship Program provides advanced training in nursing outcomes research through a formal interdisciplinary curriculum encompassing the conceptual and empirical foundations, methodological approaches, and statistical tools of health outcomes research. Fellows develop expertise in large-scale observational study design, quasi-experimental methods, and the translation of research findings into health policy.

Training is conducted within a rich collaborative environment that includes faculty, fellows, and senior researchers working across a portfolio of funded studies — giving trainees direct experience with ongoing data collection, analysis, and dissemination at the intersection of nursing science and health policy.

Fellowship Programs

T32 Fellowship

Predoctoral Training Program

Full-time doctoral training for registered nurses pursuing PhD-level research in nursing outcomes, health services, and health policy. Fellows receive mentorship, funding, and integration into active CHOPR research projects.

Learn more at CHOPR → T32 Fellowship

Postdoctoral Training Program

Two-year postdoctoral fellowships for nurses who have completed doctoral training and seek advanced preparation in outcomes research methods, grant writing, and independent research development.

Learn more at CHOPR → Mentorship Program

Clinical Nurse Research Mentorship

A structured mentorship program pairing practicing clinical nurses with CHOPR faculty and fellows to build research skills, support evidence-based practice, and develop the pipeline of nurse researchers.

Learn more at CHOPR →
Funding

Predoctoral and postdoctoral fellowships are supported by an Institutional Training Grant from the National Institute of Nursing Research (T32 NR-007104). CHOPR has maintained continuous T32 funding for over two decades, reflecting the program's national standing in nursing outcomes research training.

Research Training Areas

Fellows receive training across the substantive and methodological domains central to CHOPR's research portfolio:

Patient & nurse outcomes research
Health disparities & equity
International health systems
Nurse staffing policy evaluation
Quasi-experimental methods
Large-scale survey design
Seriously ill & high-need patients
Healthcare policy & programs

Full program details, application information, current fellows, and alumni profiles are available on the CHOPR Fellowship & Scholarship Programs page at Penn Nursing.
