About

Advancing Nursing Science
to Shape Health Policy

Matthew D. McHugh is Professor and Independence Chair for Nursing Education at the University of Pennsylvania School of Nursing, where he directs the Center for Health Outcomes and Policy Research (CHOPR). He is an elected member of the National Academy of Medicine and a Fellow of the American Academy of Nursing.

McHugh’s research examines how nursing resources—including staffing levels, work environments, and organizational factors—affect patient outcomes, nurse well-being, and health equity. His work has directly informed policy discussions at state, national, and international levels, including nurse-to-patient ratio legislation in California, Pennsylvania, New York, and Queensland, Australia.

A uniquely credentialed scholar, McHugh holds a PhD in Nursing from Penn, an MPH from Harvard, and a JD from Northeastern University School of Law—bringing disciplinary depth to the intersection of health policy, law, and nursing science. He began his career as a registered nurse and family nurse practitioner, grounding his research in clinical practice.

He is a Fulbright Scholar, former Robert Wood Johnson Foundation Nurse Faculty Scholar, and has received the Claire M. Fagin Distinguished Researcher Award and induction into the International Nurse Researcher Hall of Fame.

Center for Health Outcomes and Policy Research

Research Focus

CHOPR is a multidisciplinary research center at Penn Nursing dedicated to understanding how features of nursing—staffing, work environments, and education—shape patient outcomes and health policy. Visit CHOPR →

Nurse Staffing & Policy

Evaluating the effects of nurse-to-patient ratio legislation on staffing levels, patient mortality, readmissions, and cost—with studies in California, Pennsylvania, New York, and Queensland, Australia.

Nurse Burnout & Work Environments

Examining how organizational factors drive nurse burnout, job dissatisfaction, and intent to leave—and identifying structural interventions that create healthy work environments.

Health Equity & Disparities

Investigating how nursing resources mediate racial and ethnic disparities in surgical outcomes, cardiac arrest survival, readmissions, and care for underserved populations.

Policy Evaluation & Methods

Applying quasi-experimental methods, matched cohort designs, and causal inference to evaluate large-scale health policy interventions including the ACA Graduate Nurse Education Demonstration.
